\chapter{Inserting mathematical expressions}

Mathematical expressions can be inserted both inline and in display mode.

\section{Inline mode}

The ways to insert mathematical expressions in inline mode are
\begin{itemize}
    \item \lstinline[language=TeX]|$...$| (preferred)
    \item \lstinline[language=TeX]|\(...\)|
    \item \lstinline[language=TeX]|\begin{math}...\end{math}|
\end{itemize}
For example, \lstinline[language=TeX]|$E = m c^2$| results in $E = m c^2$.

\section{Display mode}

To insert a single equation in display mode, you can use
\begin{itemize}
    \item \lstinline[language=TeX]|\begin{equation}...\end{equation}| (preferred, numbers the equation)
    \item \lstinline[language=TeX]|\[...\]| (does not number the equation; use rarely or not at all)
\end{itemize}

For example, the code
\begin{lstlisting}[language=TeX]
\begin{equation}
    E = m c^2
\end{equation}
\end{lstlisting}
results in
\begin{equation}
    E = m c^2
\end{equation}

\subsection{Splitting a long equation into multiple lines}

A long equation can be split into multiple lines using the \lstinline[language=TeX]|multline| environment.
The lines are separated by \lstinline[language=TeX]|\\|; the first line is left-aligned, the last line is right-aligned, and the lines in between are centered.

Example:
\begin{lstlisting}[language=TeX]
\begin{multline}
    (a + b)^{10} = a^{10} + 10 a^9 b + 45 a^8 b^2 + 120 a^7 b^3 + 210 a^6 b^4 + {} \\
    {} + 252 a^5 b^5 + 210 a^4 b^6 + 120 a^3 b^7 + 45 a^2 b^8 + 10 a b^9 + b^{10}
\end{multline}
\end{lstlisting}
\begin{multline}
    (a + b)^{10} = a^{10} + 10 a^9 b + 45 a^8 b^2 + 120 a^7 b^3 + 210 a^6 b^4 + {} \\
    {} + 252 a^5 b^5 + 210 a^4 b^6 + 120 a^3 b^7 + 45 a^2 b^8 + 10 a b^9 + b^{10}
\end{multline}

An alternative (which is often more appropriate) is to use \lstinline[language=TeX]|aligned| and/or \lstinline[language=TeX]|gathered| (see Section~\ref{sec:grouping_numbers}).

\section{Grouping multiple equations}

\subsection{Equations with own numbering}

Use the \lstinline[language=TeX]|gather| and \lstinline[language=TeX]|align| environments to group multiple equations respectively without and with alignment.
Each equation will retain its own number, and you can label each one separately.

Lines are separated by \lstinline[language=TeX]|\\|, and the alignment point in the \lstinline[language=TeX]|align| environment is indicated by \lstinline[language=TeX]|&|.

Example with \lstinline[language=TeX]|gather|:
\begin{lstlisting}[language=TeX]
\begin{gather}
    a x^2 + b x + c = 0 \\
    \Delta = b^2 - 4 a c
\end{gather}
\end{lstlisting}
\begin{gather}
    a x^2 + b x + c = 0 \\
    \Delta = b^2 - 4 a c
\end{gather}

Example with \lstinline[language=TeX]|align|:
\begin{lstlisting}[language=TeX]
\begin{align}
    \cos(2 \alpha) & = \cos^2(\alpha) - \sin^2(\alpha) = \\
    & = 2 \cos^2(\alpha) - 1 = \\
    & = 1 - 2 \sin^2(\alpha)
\end{align}
\end{lstlisting}
\begin{align}
    \cos(2 \alpha) & = \cos^2 \alpha - \sin^2 \alpha = \\
    & = 2 \cos^2 \alpha - 1 = \\
    & = 1 - 2 \sin^2 \alpha
\end{align}

\subsection{Grouping equation numbers}
\label{sec:grouping_numbers}

To group the equation numbers as well, you can use \lstinline[language=TeX]|gathered| and \lstinline[language=TeX]|aligned| environments inside an \lstinline[language=TeX]|equation|, \lstinline[language=TeX]|gather|, or \lstinline[language=TeX]|align| environment.

Example with \lstinline[language=TeX]|gathered| inside \lstinline[language=TeX]|gather|:
\begin{lstlisting}[language=TeX]
\begin{gather}
    \cos^2 \alpha = \frac13 \\
\begin{gathered}
    \cos(2 \alpha) = \cos^2 \alpha - \sin^2 \alpha = \\
    = 2 \cos^2 \alpha - 1 = -\frac13
\end{gathered}
\end{gather}
\end{lstlisting}
\begin{gather}
    \cos^2 \alpha = \frac13 \\
\begin{gathered}
    \cos(2 \alpha) = \cos^2 \alpha - \sin^2 \alpha = \\
    = 2 \cos^2 \alpha - 1 = -\frac13
\end{gathered}
\end{gather}

\subsection{Equation subnumbering}

You can also use the \lstinline[language=TeX]|subequations| environment around \lstinline[language=TeX]|gather| or \lstinline[language=TeX]|align| to introduce a subnumbering:
\begin{lstlisting}[language=TeX]
\begin{subequations}
\begin{align}
    \cos(2 \alpha) & = \cos^2 \alpha - \sin^2 \alpha = \\
    & = 2 \cos^2 \alpha - 1 = \\
    & = 1 - 2 \sin^2 \alpha
\end{align}
\end{subequations}
\end{lstlisting}
